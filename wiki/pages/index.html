About 2 % of the population in Scandinavia suffer from an autoimmune response which targets a protein called "intrinsic factor" (IF). Normally, the protein binds to vitamin B12 and receptors in teh intestines enabling the uptake of the vitamin. The binding of antibodies to the IF, however, changes the conformation of the protein which results in reduced B12 uptake. This can lead to a vitamin B12 deficiency so called Biemer's disease or pernicious anaemia.
