About 2 % of the population in Scandinavia suffer from an autoimmune response which targets a protein called "intrinsic factor" (IF). Normally, the protein binds to vitamin B12 and receptors in teh intestines enabling the uptake of the vitamin. The binding of antibodies to the IF, however, changes the conformation of the protein which results in reduced B12 uptake. This can lead to a vitamin B12 deficiency so called Biemer's disease or pernicious anaemia.

In order to tackle this disease, we have developed a new treatment plan based on an improved version of IF, to which the antibodies cannot bind. This was done through a method called site-directed mutagenesis, to mask the binding site of the antibody on the IF.
This new and improved IF can then be delivered through a pill and survive in the body of patients, who’s native IF has been inactivated. This way, it restores the body’s ability to take up vitamin B12.
We used our amazing software PROMUTOR for generating every possible candidate variants of IF by mutating the amino acid(s) on a binding site (a technique called site-directed mutagenesis). Aside from our project, PROMUTOR can also create mutants for any other occasions when the sequence and the mutation sites are given by the user.
