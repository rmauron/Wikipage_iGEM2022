Gastric Intrinsic Factor (IF) is a protein found in our gut that binds to vitamin B12 available in the intestines enabling the absorption of the vitamin. About 2% of the Scandinavian population suffer from a condition called Pernicious Anaemia, where the immune system produces antibodies targeting the IF.

Binding of these antibodies to IF, changes the conformation of the protein, resulting in reduced B12 uptake. This leads to a state of deficiency in people with the current treatment being vitamin supplements.

In order to tackle this disease, we have developed a new treatment plan based on an improved version of IF, to which the antibodies cannot bind. This was done through a method called site-directed mutagenesis, changing the surface of the protein and therefore masking the binding site of the antibody on the IF.

This new and improved IF can then be delivered through a pill and survive in the body of patients, who’s native IF has been inactivated. This way, it restores the body’s ability to take up vitamin B12.

We used our amazing software ProMutor for generating every possible candidate variants of IF by mutating the amino acid(s) on the binding site. Aside from our project, this user-friendly software can also create mutants for any other occasions when the sequence and the mutation sites are given by the user.
