BBa_K4214000

This part codes for human Gastric Intrinsic Factor (GIF). We used the human sequence of the gene. The gene is located in chromosome 11q12.1. This gene is a part of cobalamin transporters group. It is essential for the absorption of vitamin B12. Mutations in this gene may lead to pernicious anaemia.

BBa_K4214001

This part codes for the same human GIF as BBa_K4214000, flanked by a signal peptide and a polyhistidine tag. The signal peptide is used in humans for secretion of intrinsic factor, while we added the His6-tag in order to facilitate purification of the protein.

BBa_K4214002

Human GIF with His6-tag. This part has the signal peptide from BBa_K4214001 removed, as it is not required for bacterial expression. This removal was performed by using primers that excluded the signal peptide. This part was used to produce and purify our Human GiF.

BBa_K4214003

LacI (BBa_J04500) and Human GIF with His6-tag. This part is a composite part from BBa_K4214002 with the addition of LacI sequence (BBa_J04500). The LacI sequence is used in order to induce our protein expression with IPTG.

BBa_K4214004

LacI (BBa_J04500) and pelB sequence (BBa_J32015) and Human GIF with His6-tag. This part is a composite part from BBa_K4214002 with the addition of pelB sequence(BBa_J32015) and LacI (BBa_J04500). The pelB sequence is used in order to express our protein in the periplasm. The enzymes in the periplasm of E.coli can add disulfide bonds in our protein, ensuring its correct conformation. This part was used to produce and purify our Human GiF.
