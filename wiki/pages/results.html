Results

The results from our experiments are shown on the following page. They are divided into Bacterial culture results and Mammalian cell culture results. The Bacterial part contains our PCR, cloning, sequencing and protein production results. The Mammalian part contains our results from mammalian cell culture western blot, fluorescence microscopy and flow cytometry results. Some abbreviations that you will meet in the text are: wtIF(wild type intrinsic factor), pDisplay mammalian expression vector( the plasmid we bought from ThermoFisher) and Wild-type plasmid is the wtIF gene into the pDisplay mammalian expression vector.

Bacterial culture results

pDisplay mammalian expression vector

We purchased from ThermoFisher Scientific our mammalian expression vector [1]. pDisplay helps the proteins to be expressed in the surface of mammalian cells. With that way, the interactors of those proteins can be identified with different assays. Some of the features of pDisplay are T7 promoter,SV40 ori, Signal peptide for transmembrane transport, HA tag, Multiple cloning site, Myc tag, PDGFR transmembrane domain, Ampicilin and Neomycin resistance. We amplified the vector in E.coli TOP10 bacteria and performed gel electrophoresis to ensure that we isolate our plasmid. We realised that apart from our vector represented as “Band of interest” detected around 5kb there was Genomic DNA Contamination since another band appeared running higher at 10kb. We proceed with gel extracting only our band of interest in order to clone our gene into the plasmid.

PCR amplification of wt-IF gblock fragment

We purchased from IDT our wtIF gene with our extra modifications. We included a His-tag for protein production and purification of our protein in bacteria. Therefore, the primers we used to amplify the wtIF gene removed the His-tag and an intrinsic signal peptide that IF had. As it is pictured in the figure above after PCR amplification the size of wtIF gene is around 1.4kb meaning that we amplified our gene. Our next step then is to perform restriction digestion with BglII and SalI-HF both in the wtIF gene but also in the pDisplay mammalian expression vector. After this step the two parts were ligated together.

wt-IF gene into the pDisplay mammalian expression vector

This is the colony PCR agarose gel electrophoresis image of the colonies screened for the wtIF fragment. The plasmids were extracted and amplified using the primers used to amplify the wtIF gene. The lone band in the H10 lane reflects the IF fragment in the solitary clone. This H10 is the clone that wtIF is successfully cloned into pDisplay mammalian expression vector.

We continued with sending the H10 clone for sequencing to ensure that our wtIF gene is inserted succesfully. The sequencing results using the forward and reverse primers yielded good quality ~800bp fragment that aligned with the in silico plasmid. This confirmed the successful cloning of the gene into the plasmid. Therefore since we knew that our gene is there we proceeded with Site Directed Mutagenesis, generating our mutant IFs.

Site Directed Mutagenesis

These are the mutants that were carried forward for testing our binding assay. We tested the validity of those mutants by sending them for sequencing. In the picture above are shown the primers that we used to create them as well as the sequencing results highlighting the substitutions. The result shows that the necessary substitutions have taken their positions in the mutant variant. Those mutants were amplified in E.coli TOP10 and forwarded for transfection in mammalian cells.

Protein production and purification of wt-IF

In order to implement our idea and have our protein into a pill, we also attempted to produce and purified the wtIF. The same procedure will be used to produce and purify the best variant from our mutants. For that reason, we used the pSB1C3 Biobrick plasmid with the LacI promotor. This promotor is inducible by IPTG and having the gene of interest, in our case wtIF, under this promotor will drive the protein expression. As a control we used the pDisplay mammalian expression vector and the pSB1C3+LacI without the wtIF. As an extra control we used the Gastric Intrinsic Factor protein so that we ensure that our antibodies detect our protein. After the induction of the protein expression with IPTG we purified our protein. During the purification we collected the cell pellet, the flow through and the eluted part of the purification column. We loaded all of our samples in the SDS-page followed by Western Blot. We used the epitope unspecific Intrinsic Factor(IF) Rabbit primary antibody [2] and Anti-Rabbit HRP as a secondary antibody. The LacI promotor is induced with 0.5 mM of IPTG and thus the wtIF is expressed. The wtIF is detected around 55-70kDa in the Lysate and Flow-through of the 0.5 mM IPTG samples but not in the Eluted parts of the purification.

Mammalian cell culture results

Cell culturing

The figures A and B successfully establish the wonderful morphology of HEK 293T cells under 100X magnification. The figures A and B are taken at varied locations in different wells. These cultured cells were used for transfection and were further used for validation studies like fluorescent microscopy and western blotting.

Western blot

Here, the Anti-Rabbit red fluorescence(1:1000) emitted is from the secondary antibody used against our epitope unspecific Intrinsic Factor(IF) Rabbit primary antibody [2]. The Anti-Mouse green fluorescence (1:1000) is emitted from the secondary antibody used against our specific Myc Mouse primary antibody [3]. The red color in the ladder is because of the PageRulerTM Prestained Protein Ladder [4]. The prestained protein ladder is seen in the 700 nm range under fluorescence and hence they appear red in the blot.

Figure A depicts the Western Blot image (fluorescent image) of our samples-Wild type plasmid, Variant 20201 plasmid and Negative control (without any plasmid) loaded at two different concentrations-15 µg and 25 µg. The figure also depicts the control housekeeping gene GAPDH at 35 kDa. The samples were also collected at two different time points 24 and 48 hours (transfection time points). The Ladder in kDa is marked along both the sides of the blot. Figure B depicts the same blot in a much enlarged view but without the control GAPDH gene.

From both Figure A and B, we observed that our protein (marked by green arrows) is being produced in Variant 20201 by the presence of light yellow bands in between 70 kDa to 55 kDa bands. The light yellow bands indicate the binding of both Myc antibody(green) and epitope unspecific IF primary antibody(red). Myc is present in the plasmid backbone and is one of the tags for confirmation for our protein. However, the streak of green bands seen in Figure B can be understood as unspecified myc binding to various other cellular proteins in HEK cells. There is no red streak of bands seen in wells designated for samples thus indicating that our epitope unspecific antibody is extremely specific to the IF and does not bind to any other cellular proteins.

It is worthy to note that we have no visible bands in negative control as well as in Wild Type plasmid in both figures. One of the reasons for no protein production in WT plasmid is due to removal of the stop codon in the near end of the WT plasmid. While we succeeded in the removal, we believe that it may have caused some changes in the plasmid which we have overlooked by not sequencing the entire plasmid.

Fluorescence microscopy

From Figure A we can confirm that our cells are adherent and in good morphology. From Figure B, with the superimposition, we can establish that the blue DAPI (4′,6-diamidino-2-phenylindole) staining worked and every cell appeared to be stained . In Figure C, we can see the Green Fluorescence Protein (GFP-which is the positive control for the transfection), confirming that our transfection worked. In Figure D, superimposing the GFP with blue DAPI was done to see the transfection efficiency visually. As one can observe, the transfection efficiency is not very high for these cells. In Figure E, in the red fluorescence spectrum, we can see our IF protein. These IF proteins were tagged with primary rabbit Anti IF epitope unspecific antibody (1:100) and with secondary anti rabbit in red fluorescence (1:400). This confirms that our IF protein is expressed, thus coinciding with our western blot results as well. Figure F displays the superimposed image of DAPI staining the nuclei (blue) and the expression of IF on the surface of Variant 20201 (red).

Flow cytometry

We also performed the flow cytometry assay in HEK293 suspension cells. The results though weren’t conclusive. The positive control was expressed and gave some signal but neither the Wild-type plasmid nor the mutants were expressed.
